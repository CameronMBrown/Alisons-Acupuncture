# Brand & Voice

## Who we design for

A local acupuncture practice in Cobourg, ON. Visitors are prospective and
returning patients — **a large share are older or have reduced vision**. Many
arrive on a phone, some looking for hours or a phone number in a hurry.

## The feeling to protect

**Professional, but humble and trustworthy — like a well-run neighbourhood small
business.** Warm and human, never corporate or clinical-cold. The design should
reassure, not impress.

Three qualities, in priority order:

1. **Legible.** If a choice trades readability for style, readability wins. This
   is why body text is large, headings are high-contrast, and nothing important
   depends on hover.
2. **Warm.** Earthy greens, soft orange/yellow accents, a friendly old-style
   serif for headings, generous whitespace, rounded corners.
3. **Calm-but-alive.** Subtle motion adds life (gentle parallax, soft reveals),
   but it is decorative and always optional — see [motion.md](./motion.md).

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use the serif (Fraunces) for headings, system sans for body. | Set long body copy in the serif or in bold. |
| Keep accent colours for emphasis and calls-to-action. | Use bright accent orange as small text (fails contrast). |
| Let text grow on large screens. | Cap headings/body at a fixed px size. |
| Keep motion subtle and honour "reduce motion". | Add motion that loops aggressively or blocks reading. |
| Reuse tokens and the `.btn` component. | Re-declare one-off sizes, colours, or button styles per section. |

## Voice (copy)

Plain, friendly, first-person ("I'm Alison"), reassuring. Short sentences.
Explain care in everyday language, not jargon. Placeholder lorem-ipsum in the
current build should be replaced with real, warm copy in Alison's voice.
